- Christ is the "Yes" to all God's promises

Key Verses:
- 2 Corinthians 1:3-4 - "Praise be to the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, the Father of compassion and the God of all comfort, who comforts us in all our troubles"

Paul opens with thanksgiving for God's comfort in suffering and explains his recent travel plans, emphasizing God's faithfulness to His promises in Christ.


### Chapter 2
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Paul's decision to avoid another painful visit
- Forgiveness and restoration of the repentant sinner
- Triumph in Christ spreads the knowledge of God
- We are the aroma of Christ to the world

Key Verses:
- 2 Corinthians 2:10-11 - "Anyone you forgive, I also forgive... in order that Satan might not outwit us"
- 2 Corinthians 2:14-15 - "He always leads us as captives in Christ's triumphal procession and uses us to spread the aroma of the knowledge of him"

Summary:
Paul urges forgiveness for a repentant offender and describes Christians as the aroma of Christ, spreading the knowledge of God wherever they go.


### Chapter 3
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- The Corinthians are Paul's letter of recommendation
- The New Covenant is written on hearts by the Spirit
- Contrast between the old covenant of law and new covenant of the Spirit
- Freedom and transformation come through the Spirit

Key Verses:
- 2 Corinthians 3:3 - "You show that you are a letter from Christ... written not with ink but with the Spirit of the living God"
- 2 Corinthians 3:17-18 - "Now the Lord is the Spirit, and where the Spirit of the Lord is, there is freedom... being transformed into his image"

Summary:
Paul contrasts the old covenant of the law with the new covenant of the Spirit, emphasizing the transforming power and freedom found in Christ.


### Chapter 4
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Ministry sustained by God's mercy and truthfulness
- Treasure in jars of clay - God's power in human weakness
- Light affliction produces eternal glory
- Focus on eternal things, not temporary troubles

Key Verses:
- 2 Corinthians 4:7 - "We have this treasure in jars of clay to show that this all-surpassing power is from God and not from us"
- 2 Corinthians 4:16-18 - "Therefore we do not lose heart... For our light and momentary troubles are achieving for us an eternal glory"

Summary:
Paul describes how God's power is displayed through human weakness and encourages believers to focus on eternal realities rather than present afflictions.

Key Verses:
- 2 Corinthians 5:7 - "For we live by faith, not by sight"
- 2 Corinthians 5:17 - "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come: The old has gone, the new is here!"

Summary:
Paul explains the hope of resurrection, the ministry of reconciliation, and calls believers to be Christ's ambassadors in the world.

### Chapter 8
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- The Macedonian churches' example of generous giving despite poverty
- Christ's example of becoming poor to make us rich
- Principles of willing and cheerful giving according to ability
- Titus and companions sent to organize the collection for Jerusalem

Key Verses:
- 2 Corinthians 8:9 - "For you know the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ, that though he was rich, yet for your sake he became poor"

Summary:
Paul encourages generous giving by pointing to Christ's sacrificial example and the Macedonian churches' cheerful generosity despite their poverty.

- God loves a cheerful giver

Summary:
Paul continues teaching on giving, emphasizing that generous sowing leads to generous reaping and that God loves cheerful givers who trust His provision.
