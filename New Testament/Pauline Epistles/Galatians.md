## Chapters

### Chapter 1
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Paul's apostolic authority directly from Christ, not man
- Rebuke of the Galatians for turning to a different gospel
- Paul's conversion and early ministry
- No other gospel exists - those preaching otherwise are accursed

Key Verses:
- Galatians 1:8 - "But even if we or an angel from heaven should preach a gospel other than the one we preached to you, let them be under God's curse!"
- Galatians 1:11-12 - "I want you to know, brothers and sisters, that the gospel I preached is not of human origin. I did not receive it from any man, nor was I taught it; rather, I received it by revelation from Jesus Christ."

Summary:
Paul defends his apostolic authority and rebukes the Galatian believers for abandoning the true gospel of grace for a false gospel of works.


### Chapter 2
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Paul's acceptance by the Jerusalem apostles
- Confrontation with Peter in Antioch over hypocrisy
- Justification by faith in Christ, not by works of the law
- The life of faith - living by faith in the Son of God

Key Verses:
- Galatians 2:16 - "Know that a person is not justified by the works of the law, but by faith in Jesus Christ"
- Galatians 2:20 - "I have been crucified with Christ and I no longer live, but Christ lives in me. The life I now live in the body, I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave himself for me."

Summary:
Paul recounts his confrontation with Peter and establishes the foundational truth that justification comes through faith in Christ alone, not by observing the law.


### Chapter 3
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Faith vs. works - Abraham justified by faith
- The law as a guardian/tutor until Christ came
- The promise to Abraham fulfilled in Christ
- All believers are children of God through faith
- Unity in Christ - no Jew or Gentile, slave or free, male or female

Key Verses:
- Galatians 3:11 - "Clearly no one who relies on the law is justified before God, because 'the righteous will live by faith'"
- Galatians 3:26-28 - "So in Christ Jesus you are all children of God through faith... There is neither Jew nor Gentile, neither slave nor free, nor is there male and female, for you are all one in Christ Jesus."

Summary:
Paul argues that Abraham was justified by faith, not works, and that the law served as a guardian until Christ came to bring salvation to all who believe.


### Chapter 4
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Adoption as sons and heirs through Christ
- The fullness of time - God sent His Son
- Allegory of Sarah and Hagar (promise vs. law)
- Paul's concern for the Galatians' spiritual condition
- Freedom in the promise, not bondage to the law

Key Verses:
- Galatians 4:4-5 - "But when the set time had fully come, God sent his Son, born of a woman, born under the law, to redeem those under the law, that we might receive adoption to sonship"
- Galatians 4:6-7 - "Because you are his sons, God sent the Spirit of his Son into our hearts, the Spirit who calls out, 'Abba, Father.' So you are no longer a slave, but God's child; and since you are his child, God has made you also an heir"

Summary:
Paul explains believers' adoption as God's children through Christ and uses the allegory of Sarah and Hagar to contrast the freedom of the gospel with the bondage of the law.


### Chapter 5
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Stand firm in freedom - don't return to slavery
- Warning against circumcision and legalism
- Faith expressing itself through love
- Fruit of the Spirit vs. acts of the flesh
- Living by the Spirit, not gratifying flesh

Key Verses:
- Galatians 5:1 - "It is for freedom that Christ has set us free. Stand firm, then, and do not let yourselves be burdened again by a yoke of slavery"
- Galatians 5:22-23 - "But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, forbearance, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness and self-control. Against such things there is no law"

Summary:
Paul calls believers to stand firm in their freedom in Christ and contrasts the works of the flesh with the fruit of the Spirit in the life of those who walk by the Spirit.


### Chapter 6
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Practical instructions for Christian living
- Bear one another's burdens
- Sowing and reaping - doing good to all people
- Paul's final warning against the false teachers
- Boasting only in the cross of Christ

Key Verses:
- Galatians 6:2 - "Carry each other's burdens, and in this way you will fulfill the law of Christ"
- Galatians 6:7-8 - "Do not be deceived: God cannot be mocked. A man reaps what he sows. Whoever sows to please their flesh, from the flesh will reap destruction; whoever sows to please the Spirit, from the Spirit will reap eternal life"
- Galatians 6:14 - "May I never boast except in the cross of our Lord Jesus Christ, through which the world has been crucified to me, and I to the world"

Summary:
Paul concludes with practical instructions for Christian living, emphasizing bearing one another's burdens, sowing to the Spirit, and boasting only in the cross of Christ.
