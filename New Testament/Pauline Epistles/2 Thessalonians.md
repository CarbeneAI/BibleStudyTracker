## Chapters

### Chapter 1
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Paul's thanksgiving for their faith and perseverance under persecution
- God's righteous judgment on those who persecute believers
- The second coming of Christ in flaming fire
- Believers will be glorified when Christ returns

Key Verses:
- 2 Thessalonians 1:3 - "We ought always to thank God for you, brothers and sisters, and rightly so, because your faith is growing more and more"
- 2 Thessalonians 1:7 - "The Lord Jesus is revealed from heaven in blazing fire with his powerful angels"

Summary:
Paul encourages the Thessalonians in their persecution and reminds them of God's righteous judgment and Christ's glorious return.


### Chapter 2
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Correction about the Day of the Lord not having already come
- The rebellion and the "man of lawlessness" must come first
- The restrainer holding back the lawless one
- God's choosing of believers for salvation and glory
- Stand firm and hold to the teachings

Key Verses:
- 2 Thessalonians 2:3 - "Don't let anyone deceive you in any way, for that day will not come until the rebellion occurs and the man of lawlessness is revealed"
- 2 Thessalonians 2:13 - "God chose you as firstfruits to be saved through the sanctifying work of the Spirit"
- 2 Thessalonians 2:15 - "Stand firm and hold fast to the teachings we passed on to you"

Summary:
Paul corrects false teaching about the Day of the Lord, explaining that certain events must occur first, including the revelation of the lawless one.


### Chapter 3
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Request for prayer for the spread of the gospel
- Warning against idleness and busybodies
- Paul's example of working for his living
- Command: "If anyone will not work, neither shall he eat"
- Instructions for dealing with the disobedient

Key Verses:
- 2 Thessalonians 3:3 - "But the Lord is faithful, and he will strengthen you and protect you from the evil one"
- 2 Thessalonians 3:10 - "If a man will not work, he shall not eat"
- 2 Thessalonians 3:13 - "Never tire of doing what is good"

Summary:
Paul addresses the problem of idleness in the church, commanding believers to work diligently and follow his example of supporting himself through labor.
