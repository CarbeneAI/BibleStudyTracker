## Chapters

### Chapter 1
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Paul writes from prison appealing to Philemon on behalf of Onesimus, a runaway slave
- Onesimus became a Christian under Paul's ministry and is now useful for the gospel
- Paul requests Philemon to receive Onesimus back not as a slave but as a beloved brother in Christ
- Paul offers to repay any debt Onesimus owes, demonstrating Christ-like intercession
- The letter beautifully illustrates forgiveness, reconciliation, and the transforming power of the gospel

Key Verses:
- Philemon 1:6 - "I pray that your partnership with us in the faith may be effective in deepening your understanding of every good thing we share for the sake of Christ"
- Philemon 1:16 - "No longer as a slave, but better than a slave, as a dear brother"
- Philemon 1:18 - "If he has done you any wrong or owes you anything, charge it to me"

Summary:
Paul's personal letter to Philemon requesting he forgive and receive back Onesimus, his former slave who became a Christian, demonstrating the gospel's power to transform relationships and transcend social boundaries.
