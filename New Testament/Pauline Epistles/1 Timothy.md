### Chapter 2
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Instructions for prayer, especially for leaders and those in authority
- God's desire for all people to be saved and know the truth
- Christ as the one mediator between God and humanity
- Guidelines for men to pray without anger or disputing
- Instructions for women's conduct, modesty, and learning with proper submission

Key Verses:
- 1 Timothy 2:3-4 - "God our Savior, who wants all people to be saved and to come to a knowledge of the truth"
- 1 Timothy 2:5-6 - "For there is one God and one mediator between God and mankind, the man Christ Jesus"
- 1 Timothy 2:8 - "I want the men everywhere to pray, lifting up holy hands without anger or disputing"

Summary:
Paul instructs believers to pray for all people, affirms God's desire for universal salvation, and provides guidelines for worship and conduct in the church.


### Chapter 3
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Qualifications for overseers/bishops in the church
- Requirements include being above reproach, temperate, self-controlled, and respectable
- Qualifications for deacons, emphasizing integrity and tested faith
- The church described as the pillar and foundation of truth
- The mystery of godliness centered on Christ's incarnation, vindication, and exaltation

Key Verses:
- 1 Timothy 3:2-3 - "Now the overseer is to be above reproach, faithful to his wife, temperate, self-controlled, respectable, hospitable, able to teach"
- 1 Timothy 3:15 - "The church of the living God, the pillar and foundation of the truth"
- 1 Timothy 3:16 - "Beyond all question, the mystery from which true godliness springs is great"

Summary:
Paul outlines the qualifications for church leaders (overseers and deacons) and affirms the church's role as the foundation of truth in proclaiming the gospel mystery.


### Chapter 4
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Warning about false teachers who will abandon the faith in later times
- Rejection of ascetic practices that prohibit marriage and certain foods
- Everything God created is good and should be received with thanksgiving
- Instructions for Timothy to be an example in speech, conduct, love, faith, and purity
- Emphasis on training in godliness over physical training
- Timothy's spiritual gifts received through prophecy and laying on of hands

Key Verses:
- 1 Timothy 4:8 - "For physical training is of some value, but godliness has value for all things"
- 1 Timothy 4:12 - "Don't let anyone look down on you because you are young, but set an example for the believers in speech, in conduct, in love, in faith and in purity"
- 1 Timothy 4:16 - "Watch your life and doctrine closely. Persevere in them, because if you do, you will save both yourself and your hearers"

Summary:
Paul warns against false teaching and asceticism while encouraging Timothy to be an example in godliness and to faithfully exercise his spiritual gifts.


### Chapter 5
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Instructions for how to treat different age groups and genders in the church family
- Guidelines for caring for widows, distinguishing between those truly in need and those with family support
- Qualifications for widows to be enrolled for church support
- Instructions for elders, especially those who preach and teach
- Process for addressing accusations against elders
- Warning against favoritism and hasty ordination

Key Verses:
- 1 Timothy 5:8 - "Anyone who does not provide for their relatives, and especially for their own household, has denied the faith"
- 1 Timothy 5:17 - "The elders who direct the affairs of the church well are worthy of double honor, especially those whose work is preaching and teaching"
- 1 Timothy 5:22 - "Do not be hasty in the laying on of hands, and do not share in the sins of others"

Summary:
Paul provides practical instructions for caring for different groups in the church, particularly widows and elders, emphasizing family responsibility and careful leadership selection.


### Chapter 6
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Instructions for slaves to honor their masters and avoid bringing reproach on God's name
- Warning against false teachers motivated by greed and controversy

- Timothy charged to pursue righteousness, godliness, faith, love, endurance, and gentleness
- Final charge to guard what has been entrusted and avoid worldly philosophies

Key Verses:
- 1 Timothy 6:6 - "But godliness with contentment is great gain"
- 1 Timothy 6:10 - "For the love of money is a root of all kinds of evil"
- 1 Timothy 6:11-12 - "But you, man of God, flee from all this, and pursue righteousness, godliness, faith, love, endurance and gentleness. Fight the good fight of the faith"
- 1 Timothy 6:20 - "Guard what has been entrusted to your care"

Summary:
Paul concludes with warnings about greed and the love of money, charging Timothy to pursue godliness, fight the good fight of faith, and guard the truth entrusted to him.
