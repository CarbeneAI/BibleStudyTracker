## Chapters

### Chapter 1
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Trials produce perseverance and maturity
- Ask God for wisdom in faith without doubting
- Humble circumstances vs. wealth - both temporary
- Resisting temptation brings the crown of life
- Hearing and doing the Word, not just hearing

Key Verses:
- James 1:2-3 - "Consider it pure joy, my brothers and sisters, whenever you face trials of many kinds, because you know that the testing of your faith produces perseverance"
- James 1:5 - "If any of you lacks wisdom, you should ask God, who gives generously to all without finding fault"
- James 1:22 - "Do not merely listen to the word, and so deceive yourselves. Do what it says"

Summary:
James encourages believers to joyfully endure trials, ask God for wisdom, and be doers of the Word rather than hearers only.


### Chapter 2
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Warning against favoritism and partiality
- The royal law: love your neighbor as yourself
- Faith without works is dead
- Abraham and Rahab as examples of faith in action
- Genuine faith produces corresponding actions

Key Verses:
- James 2:8 - "If you really keep the royal law found in Scripture, 'Love your neighbor as yourself,' you are doing right"
- James 2:17 - "In the same way, faith by itself, if it is not accompanied by action, is dead"
- James 2:26 - "As the body without the spirit is dead, so faith without deeds is dead"

Summary:
James condemns showing favoritism and teaches that authentic faith must be demonstrated through works, not words alone.


### Chapter 3
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Teachers face stricter judgment
- The power and danger of the tongue
- Small things control great things (bit, rudder, spark)
- The tongue can bless or curse
- Wisdom from above vs. earthly wisdom

Key Verses:
- James 3:5-6 - "Likewise, the tongue is a small part of the body, but it makes great boasts. Consider what a great forest is set on fire by a small spark"
- James 3:10 - "Out of the same mouth come praise and cursing. My brothers and sisters, this should not be"
- James 3:17 - "But the wisdom that comes from heaven is first of all pure; then peace-loving, considerate, submissive, full of mercy and good fruit"

Summary:
James warns about the destructive power of the tongue and contrasts worldly wisdom with the pure, peaceful wisdom from above.


### Chapter 4
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Quarrels come from selfish desires
- Friendship with the world is enmity with God
- Submit to God and resist the devil
- Humility before God brings exaltation
- Don't judge others or boast about tomorrow

Key Verses:
- James 4:6 - "God opposes the proud but shows favor to the humble"
- James 4:7-8 - "Submit yourselves, then, to God. Resist the devil, and he will flee from you. Come near to God and he will come near to you"
- James 4:14 - "Why, you do not even know what will happen tomorrow. What is your life? You are a mist that appears for a little while and then vanishes"

Summary:
James calls believers to humble submission to God, warning against worldliness, pride, and arrogant plans for the future.


### Chapter 5
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Warning to the rich who exploit workers
- Patient endurance while waiting for the Lord's return
- Don't swear oaths - let your yes be yes
- Prayer, confession, and healing in the community
- Bringing back wandering believers

Key Verses:
- James 5:7-8 - "Be patient, then, brothers and sisters, until the Lord's coming. See how the farmer waits for the land to yield its valuable crop"
- James 5:16 - "Therefore confess your sins to each other and pray for each other so that you may be healed. The prayer of a righteous person is powerful and effective"
- James 5:19-20 - "Whoever turns a sinner from the error of their way will save them from death and cover over a multitude of sins"

Summary:
James concludes with encouragement to be patient in suffering, pray earnestly for one another, and actively pursue restoration of those who wander from the truth.
