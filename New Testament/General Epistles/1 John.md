## Chapters

### Chapter 1
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- John testifies about the Word of Life that was from the beginning - Jesus Christ incarnate
- Fellowship with God requires walking in the light, not darkness
- God is light and in Him is no darkness at all
- Honest confession of sin leads to cleansing and forgiveness through Jesus' blood
- Claiming to be without sin makes God a liar and shows self-deception

Key Verses:
- 1 John 1:5 - "God is light; in him there is no darkness at all"
- 1 John 1:7 - "But if we walk in the light, as he is in the light, we have fellowship with one another, and the blood of Jesus, his Son, purifies us from all sin"
- 1 John 1:9 - "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just and will forgive us our sins and purify us from all unrighteousness"

Summary:
John establishes the reality of Christ's incarnation and teaches that true fellowship with God requires walking in the light and honestly confessing our sins.

### Chapter 2
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Jesus Christ is our advocate with the Father when we sin
- Obedience to God's commands is the proof of knowing Him
- The new command is to love one another as Jesus loved us
- Warning against loving the world and its temporary desires
- False teachers (antichrists) deny that Jesus is the Christ

Key Verses:
- 1 John 2:1-2 - "My dear children, I write this to you so that you will not sin. But if anybody does sin, we have an advocate with the Father—Jesus Christ, the Righteous One"
- 1 John 2:15 - "Do not love the world or anything in the world. If anyone loves the world, love for the Father is not in them"
- 1 John 2:27 - "The anointing you received from him remains in you, and you do not need anyone to teach you"

Summary:
John teaches about Christ as our advocate, emphasizes obedience and love as proof of knowing God, and warns against worldliness and false teachers who deny Christ.

### Chapter 3
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- God's incredible love makes us His children, though the world doesn't recognize us
- When Christ appears, we will be like Him and see Him as He is
- Those born of God do not continue in sin; they practice righteousness
- The Devil's children are distinguished from God's children by their lack of love
- True love is demonstrated through actions, not just words - following Christ's example of laying down His life

Key Verses:
- 1 John 3:1 - "See what great love the Father has lavished on us, that we should be called children of God! And that is what we are!"
- 1 John 3:16 - "This is how we know what love is: Jesus Christ laid down his life for us. And we ought to lay down our lives for our brothers and sisters"
- 1 John 3:18 - "Dear children, let us not love with words or speech but with actions and in truth"

Summary:
John emphasizes the transformative identity of being God's children, contrasts those who practice righteousness versus sin, and calls believers to love sacrificially through concrete actions.

### Chapter 4
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Test every spirit - true prophets confess Jesus Christ came in the flesh
- God is love, demonstrated by sending His Son as atoning sacrifice for our sins
- We love because He first loved us; love for God must result in love for others
- Perfect love drives out fear of judgment because love has been perfected in us
- Anyone claiming to love God while hating their brother is a liar

Key Verses:
- 1 John 4:8 - "Whoever does not love does not know God, because God is love"
- 1 John 4:10 - "This is love: not that we loved God, but that he loved us and sent his Son as an atoning sacrifice for our sins"
- 1 John 4:18 - "There is no fear in love. But perfect love drives out fear, because fear has to do with punishment"

Summary:
John instructs believers to test spirits to discern truth from error, reveals that God's very nature is love demonstrated through Christ's sacrifice, and teaches that genuine love for God produces fearless confidence and love for others.

### Chapter 5
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Everyone born of God believes Jesus is the Christ and overcomes the world through faith
- Three witnesses testify about Jesus: the Spirit, the water, and the blood
- God has given us eternal life through His Son - whoever has the Son has life
- We can pray with confidence knowing God hears us when we ask according to His will
- We know that anyone born of God does not continue to sin; the evil one cannot harm them

Key Verses:
- 1 John 5:4 - "For everyone born of God overcomes the world. This is the victory that has overcome the world, even our faith"
- 1 John 5:11-12 - "And this is the testimony: God has given us eternal life, and this life is in his Son. Whoever has the Son has life; whoever does not have the Son of God does not have life"
- 1 John 5:14 - "This is the confidence we have in approaching God: that if we ask anything according to his will, he hears us"

Summary:
John concludes by affirming that faith in Jesus Christ as God's Son brings victory over the world, eternal life, and confident access to God in prayer, while emphasizing the testimony of the Spirit confirming Christ's identity.
