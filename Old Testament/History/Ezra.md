## Chapters

### Chapter 1
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Cyrus's decree allows Jews to return to Jerusalem
- Fulfillment of Jeremiah's 70-year prophecy
- God stirs the hearts of the exiles to return
- Temple vessels restored by Cyrus

Key Verses:
- Ezra 1:1 - "In the first year of Cyrus king of Persia, in order to fulfill the word of the Lord spoken by Jeremiah"
- Ezra 1:3 - "Any of his people among you may go up to Jerusalem in Judah and build the temple"
- Ezra 1:5 - "Then... everyone whose heart God had moved—prepared to go up and build the house of the Lord"

Summary:
King Cyrus decrees the return of Jewish exiles and restoration of temple treasures, fulfilling God's prophetic word through Jeremiah.


### Chapter 2
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Complete census of returning exiles (42,360 people)
- List organized by family, city, and occupation
- Priests, Levites, singers, and temple servants enumerated
- Generous freewill offerings for the temple rebuilding

Key Verses:
- Ezra 2:64 - "The whole company numbered 42,360"
- Ezra 2:68-69 - "Some of the heads of the families... gave freewill offerings toward the rebuilding of the house of God"
- Ezra 2:70 - "The priests, the Levites, the musicians... settled in their own towns"

Summary:
A detailed record of nearly 50,000 people who returned from Babylon to rebuild Jerusalem and the temple.


### Chapter 3
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Altar rebuilt and sacrifices resume before temple construction
- Foundation of the temple laid in the second year
- Mixed emotions: joy and weeping at the ceremony
- Feast of Tabernacles celebrated

Key Verses:
- Ezra 3:3 - "Despite their fear of the peoples around them, they built the altar"
- Ezra 3:11 - "With praise and thanksgiving they sang to the Lord: 'He is good; his love toward Israel endures forever'"
- Ezra 3:12-13 - "Many of the older priests... wept aloud, while many others shouted for joy"

Summary:
The returning exiles rebuild the altar and lay the temple foundation amid both celebration and sorrowful remembrance of Solomon's temple.


### Chapter 4
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Opposition from local enemies who offer deceptive help
- Enemies hire counselors to frustrate the work
- Letter to King Artaxerxes accusing Jews of rebellion
- Work on the temple stopped until Darius's reign

Key Verses:
- Ezra 4:4-5 - "Then the peoples around them set out to discourage the people of Judah and make them afraid to go on building"
- Ezra 4:21 - "Now issue an order to these men to stop work"
- Ezra 4:24 - "Thus the work on the house of God in Jerusalem came to a standstill"

Summary:
Enemy opposition through deception, intimidation, and false accusations halts temple construction for about 15 years.


### Chapter 5
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Prophets Haggai and Zechariah encourage resumed construction
- Governor Tattenai investigates but doesn't stop the work
- Letter sent to King Darius requesting verification of Cyrus's decree
- God's eye watches over the elders during investigation

Key Verses:
- Ezra 5:1-2 - "Now Haggai the prophet and Zechariah... prophesied to the Jews... Then... they began to rebuild"
- Ezra 5:5 - "But the eye of their God was watching over the elders of the Jews"
- Ezra 5:11 - "We are the servants of the God of heaven and earth"

Summary:
Through prophetic encouragement, the Jews resume building despite questioning from Persian officials, trusting God's protection.


### Chapter 6
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Cyrus's original decree discovered in Ecbatana archives
- Darius orders full support for temple completion
- Temple finished and dedicated with great celebration
- Passover celebrated by the returning exiles

Key Verses:
- Ezra 6:12 - "May God, who has caused his name to dwell there, overthrow any king or people who lifts a hand to change this"
- Ezra 6:14 - "So the elders of the Jews continued to build and prosper under the preaching of Haggai... and Zechariah"
- Ezra 6:22 - "For seven days they celebrated... For the Lord had filled them with joy"

Summary:
King Darius confirms Cyrus's decree, supports the temple completion, and the Jews celebrate with dedication and Passover.


### Chapter 7
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Ezra introduced as priest and scribe skilled in the Law
- Journey from Babylon to Jerusalem (about 60 years after first return)
- King Artaxerxes grants Ezra's requests and provides resources
- Ezra's heart set on studying, obeying, and teaching God's Law

Key Verses:
- Ezra 7:10 - "For Ezra had devoted himself to the study and observance of the Law of the Lord, and to teaching its decrees"
- Ezra 7:6 - "The hand of the Lord his God was on him"
- Ezra 7:27-28 - "Praise be to the Lord... who has extended his good favor to me before the king"

Summary:
Ezra, a devoted scribe of God's Law, receives royal support from Artaxerxes to lead another group to Jerusalem and teach the people.


### Chapter 8
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- List of family heads returning with Ezra (about 1,500 men plus families)
- Ezra recruits Levites at Ahava Canal for temple service
- Fasting and prayer for safe journey instead of requesting military escort
- Valuable temple treasures entrusted to priests for transport

Key Verses:
- Ezra 8:21-22 - "I proclaimed a fast... so that we might humble ourselves before our God... I was ashamed to ask the king for soldiers"
- Ezra 8:31 - "The hand of our God was on us, and he protected us from enemies"
- Ezra 8:28 - "You as well as these articles are consecrated to the Lord"

Summary:
Ezra leads another wave of returnees, trusting God's protection through prayer and fasting rather than military escort.


### Chapter 9
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Leaders report widespread intermarriage with pagan nations
- Ezra responds with intense grief, tearing clothes and pulling hair
- Powerful prayer of confession despite personal innocence
- Acknowledges God's grace in preserving a remnant

Key Verses:
- Ezra 9:2 - "They have taken some of their daughters as wives for themselves and their sons, and have mingled the holy race"
- Ezra 9:6 - "I am too ashamed and disgraced, my God, to lift up my face to you"
- Ezra 9:13-14 - "What has happened to us is a result of our evil deeds... Shall we then break your commands again?"

Summary:
Ezra discovers widespread intermarriage violations and leads the people in heartbroken confession before God.


### Chapter 10
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- People gather in repentance, trembling before God
- Covenant made to separate from foreign wives
- Commission investigates each case over three months
- List of 113 men who had married foreign women

Key Verses:
- Ezra 10:1 - "While Ezra was praying... a large crowd of Israelites... gathered around him. They too wept bitterly"
- Ezra 10:11 - "Now honor the Lord, the God of your ancestors, and do his will. Separate yourselves from the peoples around you"
- Ezra 10:44 - "All these had married foreign women, and some of them had children by these wives"

Summary:
The people covenant to separate from foreign wives to remain faithful to God's Law, demonstrating costly obedience and repentance.
