## Chapters

### Chapter 1
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- King Xerxes displays his wealth in a 180-day celebration
- Queen Vashti refuses the king's command to appear before the banquet guests
- Vashti is deposed as queen for her disobedience
- The king's advisors fear other wives will follow Vashti's example

Key Verses:
- Esther 1:12 - "But when the attendants delivered the king's command, Queen Vashti refused to come"
- Esther 1:19 - "Let the king give her royal position to someone else who is better than she"

Summary:
Queen Vashti's refusal to obey King Xerxes leads to her removal and sets the stage for a new queen to be chosen.


### Chapter 2
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Search for a new queen begins throughout the Persian Empire
- Esther, a Jewish orphan raised by Mordecai, is taken to the palace
- Esther finds favor and wins the beauty contest, becoming queen
- Mordecai uncovers an assassination plot against the king

Key Verses:
- Esther 2:17 - "Now the king was attracted to Esther more than to any of the other women, and she won his favor and approval"
- Esther 2:10 - "Esther had not revealed her nationality and family background, because Mordecai had forbidden her to do so"

Summary:
Esther becomes queen of Persia while keeping her Jewish identity secret, and Mordecai saves the king's life.


### Chapter 3
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Haman is promoted to highest position in the kingdom
- Mordecai refuses to bow to Haman, enraging him
- Haman plots to destroy all Jews throughout the Persian Empire
- The king issues a decree to annihilate the Jews on a specific date

Key Verses:
- Esther 3:6 - "Yet having learned who Mordecai's people were, he scorned the idea of killing only Mordecai. Instead Haman looked for a way to destroy all Mordecai's people"
- Esther 3:13 - "Dispatches were sent by couriers to all the king's provinces with the order to destroy, kill and annihilate all the Jews"

Summary:
Haman's hatred for Mordecai leads him to deceive the king into issuing a decree for the genocide of all Jews.


### Chapter 4
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Mordecai mourns in sackcloth and ashes over the decree
- He urges Esther to intercede with the king for her people
- Esther hesitates, knowing she could be killed for approaching the king uninvited
- Mordecai's famous words about being in the kingdom "for such a time as this"

Key Verses:
- Esther 4:14 - "And who knows but that you have come to your royal position for such a time as this?"
- Esther 4:16 - "I will go to the king, even though it is against the law. And if I perish, I perish"

Summary:
Mordecai challenges Esther to risk her life by approaching the king, and she courageously accepts the mission.


### Chapter 5
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Esther approaches the king and is received favorably
- She invites the king and Haman to two banquets
- Haman is thrilled but still enraged by Mordecai's refusal to bow
- Haman builds a gallows to hang Mordecai

Key Verses:
- Esther 5:3 - "What is it, Queen Esther? What is your request? Even up to half the kingdom, it will be given you"
- Esther 5:14 - "Have a pole set up, reaching to a height of fifty cubits, and ask the king in the morning to have Mordecai impaled on it"

Summary:
Esther gains the king's favor and begins her strategic plan, while Haman prepares to execute Mordecai.


### Chapter 6
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- The king has insomnia and reviews the royal records
- He discovers Mordecai's unrewarded act of saving his life
- Haman arrives to request Mordecai's execution but is asked how to honor someone
- Haman must publicly honor Mordecai, the very man he planned to kill

Key Verses:
- Esther 6:6 - "Haman thought to himself, 'Who is there that the king would rather honor than me?'"
- Esther 6:11 - "So Haman got the robe and the horse. He robed Mordecai, and led him on horseback through the city streets"

Summary:
In a divine twist, Haman is forced to publicly honor Mordecai instead of executing him.


### Chapter 7
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- At the second banquet, Esther reveals her Jewish identity
- She exposes Haman's plot to destroy her people
- The king is furious and orders Haman's execution
- Haman is hanged on the very gallows he built for Mordecai

Key Verses:
- Esther 7:3-4 - "If I have found favor with you, Your Majesty, and if it pleases you, grant me my life—this is my petition. And spare my people—this is my request"
- Esther 7:10 - "So they impaled Haman on the pole he had set up for Mordecai. Then the king's fury subsided"

Summary:
Esther courageously reveals Haman's evil plot, resulting in Haman's execution on his own gallows.


### Chapter 8
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- The king gives Haman's estate to Esther and elevates Mordecai
- The original decree cannot be revoked due to Persian law
- A new decree is issued allowing Jews to defend themselves
- Jews throughout the empire rejoice at the counter-decree

Key Verses:
- Esther 8:8 - "Now write another decree in the king's name in behalf of the Jews as seems best to you, and seal it with the king's signet ring"
- Esther 8:17 - "In every province and in every city to which the edict of the king came, there was joy and gladness among the Jews"

Summary:
The king issues a counter-decree allowing Jews to defend themselves, transforming sorrow into joy.


### Chapter 9
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:

Key Verses:
- Esther 9:22 - "As the time when the Jews got relief from their enemies, and as the month when their sorrow was turned into joy"

Summary:
The Jews successfully defend themselves and establish the annual Festival of Purim to celebrate their deliverance.
