## Chapters

### Chapter 1
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- God's judgment announced against Samaria and Jerusalem
- Micah's lament over the coming destruction
- Sins of idolatry and injustice exposed
- Judgment depicted as God coming down to tread upon the earth

Key Verses:
- Micah 1:2 - "Hear, you peoples, all of you, listen, earth and all who live in it"
- Micah 1:3-4 - "Look! The Lord is coming from his dwelling place"

Summary:
Micah prophesies God's judgment on both Israel and Judah for their idolatry and injustice, with vivid imagery of divine intervention.


### Chapter 2
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Condemnation of those who oppress the poor and seize property
- False prophets denounced for preaching lies
- Promise of future restoration and gathering of God's people
- God will break through as their king and leader

Key Verses:
- Micah 2:1-2 - "Woe to those who plan iniquity, to those who plot evil on their beds"
- Micah 2:12-13 - "I will surely gather all of you, Jacob; I will surely bring together the remnant"

Summary:
Micah condemns social injustice and false prophets while promising God will gather and restore His scattered people.


### Chapter 3
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Leaders who oppress the people compared to cannibals
- Prophets who lead people astray for personal gain
- Micah contrasts himself as filled with God's Spirit
- Jerusalem and temple will be destroyed because of corruption

Key Verses:
- Micah 3:8 - "But as for me, I am filled with power, with the Spirit of the Lord"
- Micah 3:12 - "Therefore because of you, Zion will be plowed like a field"

Summary:
Micah condemns corrupt leaders, priests, and prophets who exploit the people, declaring Jerusalem's coming destruction.


### Chapter 4
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Vision of the mountain of the Lord exalted in the last days
- Nations streaming to learn God's ways and walk in His paths
- Prophecy of universal peace - swords into plowshares
- Promise of God's reign and restoration of His people

Key Verses:
- Micah 4:2-3 - "He will teach us his ways, so that we may walk in his paths"
- Micah 4:3 - "They will beat their swords into plowshares and their spears into pruning hooks"

Summary:
Micah prophesies the future exaltation of God's kingdom where nations will seek Him and experience lasting peace.


### Chapter 5
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Prophecy of Messiah's birthplace in Bethlehem
- The ruler from ancient times who will shepherd Israel
- Deliverance from Assyria and future enemies
- Purification of Israel from idols and false worship

Key Verses:
- Micah 5:2 - "But you, Bethlehem Ephrathah, though you are small among the clans of Judah, out of you will come for me one who will be ruler over Israel"
- Micah 5:4 - "He will stand and shepherd his flock in the strength of the Lord"

Summary:
Micah prophesies the Messiah's birth in Bethlehem and His role as shepherd-king who will bring peace and deliverance.


### Chapter 6
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- God's controversy with His people presented as courtroom drama
- Reminder of God's faithfulness and deliverance from Egypt
- Famous statement of what God requires - justice, mercy, and humility
- Condemnation of dishonest business practices and violence

Key Verses:
- Micah 6:8 - "He has shown you, O mortal, what is good. And what does the Lord require of you? To act justly and to love mercy and to walk humbly with your God"
- Micah 6:4 - "I brought you up out of Egypt and redeemed you from the land of slavery"

Summary:
God calls His people to account, defining true worship as justice, mercy, and humility rather than empty rituals.


### Chapter 7
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Micah laments widespread corruption and broken relationships
- Trust in God even when society collapses
- Confidence in God's compassion and faithfulness to forgive
- Promise that God will pardon sin and show mercy

Key Verses:
- Micah 7:18-19 - "Who is a God like you, who pardons sin and forgives the transgression of the remnant of his inheritance?"
- Micah 7:7 - "But as for me, I watch in hope for the Lord, I wait for God my Savior"

Summary:
Despite pervasive evil, Micah expresses hope in God's mercy and celebrates His incomparable compassion and faithfulness to forgive.
