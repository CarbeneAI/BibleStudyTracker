## Chapters

### Chapter 1
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Amos, a shepherd from Tekoa, prophesies judgment on surrounding nations
- God pronounces judgment on Damascus, Gaza, Tyre, Edom, Ammon, and Moab
- Each nation is judged for specific sins of violence and cruelty
- Pattern of "for three sins... even for four" emphasizes completeness of guilt

Key Verses:
- Amos 1:1 - "The words of Amos, one of the shepherds of Tekoa"
- Amos 1:2 - "The Lord roars from Zion and thunders from Jerusalem"

Summary:
Amos begins his prophecy by pronouncing God's judgment on Israel's neighboring nations for their sins of violence and treaty violations.


### Chapter 2
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Judgment on Judah for rejecting God's law
- Extended judgment oracle against Israel for social injustice
- Israelites oppress the poor and pervert righteousness
- God reminds them of His deliverance from Egypt and provision
- No escape for the strong when judgment comes

Key Verses:
- Amos 2:6-7 - "They sell the innocent for silver, and the needy for a pair of sandals"
- Amos 2:10 - "I brought you up out of Egypt and led you forty years in the wilderness"

Summary:
God pronounces judgment on Judah and especially Israel for rejecting His law and oppressing the poor despite His gracious deliverance and provision.


### Chapter 3
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- God's special relationship with Israel brings greater accountability
- Series of rhetorical questions about cause and effect
- God reveals His plans to His prophets before acting
- Judgment will come on Israel's luxury and altars
- Witnesses called to observe Israel's sins and coming destruction

Key Verses:
- Amos 3:2 - "You only have I chosen of all the families of the earth; therefore I will punish you for all your sins"
- Amos 3:7 - "Surely the Sovereign Lord does nothing without revealing his plan to his servants the prophets"

Summary:
Israel's privileged position as God's chosen people makes them more accountable, and God reveals through His prophets the coming judgment for their sins.


### Chapter 4
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Condemnation of wealthy women who oppress the poor ("cows of Bashan")
- God sent warnings through famine, drought, plagues, and military defeats
- Despite repeated warnings, Israel refused to return to God
- Refrain "yet you have not returned to me" repeated five times
- Solemn call to "prepare to meet your God, Israel"

Key Verses:
- Amos 4:1 - "You cows of Bashan on Mount Samaria, you women who oppress the poor"
- Amos 4:12 - "Prepare to meet your God, Israel"

Summary:
Despite God's repeated warnings through various calamities, Israel refused to repent, prompting the ominous call to prepare for divine judgment.


### Chapter 5
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Lament for fallen Israel - "virgin Israel has fallen"
- Call to seek the Lord and live
- God rejects their religious festivals and sacrifices
- Famous call for justice and righteousness
- The coming "day of the Lord" will be darkness, not light

Key Verses:
- Amos 5:4 - "Seek me and live"
- Amos 5:24 - "But let justice roll on like a river, righteousness like a never-failing stream!"
- Amos 5:21-22 - "I hate, I despise your religious festivals"

Summary:
God calls Israel to seek Him genuinely rather than offering empty religious rituals, demanding justice and righteousness instead of hypocritical worship.


### Chapter 6
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Woe to those complacent in Zion and secure in Samaria
- Leaders live in luxury while ignoring the nation's ruin
- They indulge in feasting, music, and wine without concern
- Pride of Jacob will be delivered up to enemies
- The entire nation from Lebo Hamath to the valley of Arabah will be oppressed

Key Verses:
- Amos 6:1 - "Woe to you who are complacent in Zion, and to you who feel secure on Mount Samaria"
- Amos 6:6 - "But you do not grieve over the ruin of Joseph"
- Amos 6:8 - "The Lord God Almighty declares: 'I abhor the pride of Jacob'"

Summary:
God pronounces woe on Israel's complacent leaders who live in self-indulgent luxury while remaining indifferent to the nation's spiritual decay and coming destruction.


### Chapter 7
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Five visions: locusts, fire, and plumb line
- Amos intercedes and God relents on first two judgments
- Vision of plumb line shows Israel's moral crookedness
- Amaziah the priest opposes Amos and tells him to leave
- Amos declares he is not a professional prophet but called by God
- Judgment pronounced on Amaziah and his family

Key Verses:
- Amos 7:8 - "I am setting a plumb line among my people Israel"
- Amos 7:14-15 - "I was neither a prophet nor the son of a prophet... But the Lord took me from tending the flock"

Summary:
Through visions of judgment and confrontation with priest Amaziah, Amos affirms his divine calling and declares that Israel has failed God's standard of righteousness.


### Chapter 8
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Vision of a basket of ripe fruit symbolizing Israel's end
- Merchants condemned for exploiting the poor
- They use dishonest scales and sell inferior products
- Coming judgment includes darkness, mourning, and famine
- Famine not of bread but of hearing the words of the Lord

Key Verses:
- Amos 8:2 - "The time is ripe for my people Israel; I will spare them no longer"
- Amos 8:11 - "I will send a famine through the land—not a famine of food or a thirst for water, but a famine of hearing the words of the Lord"

Summary:
The vision of ripe fruit signals Israel's time of judgment has come, and God will send a spiritual famine where His word will no longer be heard.


### Chapter 9
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Vision of the Lord standing by the altar commanding destruction
- No one will escape God's judgment - even if they hide
- God's eyes are on the sinful kingdom to destroy it
- Promise that Israel will not be totally destroyed
- Future restoration: David's fallen tent will be rebuilt
- Promises of abundant prosperity and permanent restoration

Key Verses:
- Amos 9:11 - "In that day I will restore David's fallen shelter—I will repair its broken walls and restore its ruins"
- Amos 9:13 - "The days are coming... when the reaper will be overtaken by the plowman"
- Amos 9:15 - "I will plant Israel in their own land, never again to be uprooted"

Summary:
Despite certain judgment on sinful Israel, God promises future restoration of David's kingdom with abundant blessing and permanent security for His people.
