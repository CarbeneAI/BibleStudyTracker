## Chapters

### Chapter 1
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- God commands Hosea to marry Gomer, a promiscuous woman
- The marriage symbolizes Israel's spiritual adultery against God
- Three children born with symbolic names: Jezreel (judgment), Lo-Ruhamah (no mercy), Lo-Ammi (not my people)
- Prophetic imagery of Israel's unfaithfulness and coming judgment

Key Verses:
- Hosea 1:2 - "When the Lord began to speak through Hosea, the Lord said to him, 'Go, marry a promiscuous woman'"
- Hosea 1:9 - "Then the Lord said: 'Call him Lo-Ammi (not my people), for you are not my people'"

Summary:
God commands Hosea to marry an unfaithful woman as a living metaphor of Israel's spiritual adultery, and their children receive prophetic names foretelling judgment.


### Chapter 2
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- God's lawsuit against unfaithful Israel for pursuing false gods
- Israel chases after lovers (Baal worship) instead of the true God
- God will discipline Israel by removing blessings, then restore her
- Promise of renewal and a new covenant of faithfulness

Key Verses:
- Hosea 2:14-15 - "Therefore I am now going to allure her; I will lead her into the wilderness and speak tenderly to her"
- Hosea 2:23 - "I will say to those called 'Not my people,' 'You are my people'; and they will say, 'You are my God'"

Summary:
God confronts Israel's spiritual adultery and idolatry, promising both discipline and eventual restoration through His unfailing love.


### Chapter 3
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- God commands Hosea to love his unfaithful wife again
- Hosea buys Gomer back from slavery/prostitution
- Period of waiting and purification before restoration
- Parallel to Israel's exile and eventual return to God

Key Verses:
- Hosea 3:1 - "The Lord said to me, 'Go, show your love to your wife again, though she is loved by another'"
- Hosea 3:5 - "Afterward the Israelites will return and seek the Lord their God and David their king"

Summary:
Hosea redeems his unfaithful wife, illustrating God's persistent love and His plan to restore Israel after a period of discipline.


### Chapter 4
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- God's indictment against Israel for lack of faithfulness, love, and knowledge
- Priests have failed in their responsibility to teach God's law
- Widespread idolatry and sexual immorality linked to Baal worship
- People destroyed for lack of knowledge of God

Key Verses:
- Hosea 4:6 - "My people are destroyed from lack of knowledge"
- Hosea 4:1 - "There is no faithfulness, no love, no acknowledgment of God in the land"

Summary:
God charges Israel with covenant unfaithfulness, particularly blaming the priests for failing to teach the people, resulting in widespread ignorance and sin.


### Chapter 5
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Judgment pronounced on priests, Israel, and Judah's leaders
- Israel's pride and arrogance testify against them
- Attempts at repentance are shallow and insincere
- God will withdraw until they acknowledge their guilt

Key Verses:
- Hosea 5:4 - "Their deeds do not permit them to return to their God"
- Hosea 5:15 - "Then I will return to my lair until they have borne their guilt and seek my face"

Summary:
God condemns the religious and political leaders for leading Israel astray and declares He will withdraw until genuine repentance occurs.


### Chapter 6
- [ ] Read 📖 2025-MM-DD
Notes:
- Call to return to the Lord with expectation of healing
- God desires mercy and knowledge of Him, not mere sacrifices
- Israel's love is fleeting like morning mist
- Catalog of crimes committed in various cities

Key Verses:
- Hosea 6:6 - "For I desire mercy, not sacrifice, and acknowledgment of God rather than burnt offerings"
- Hosea 6:1 - "Come, let us return to the Lord. He has torn us to pieces but he will heal us"

Summary:
God calls for genuine repentance rather than empty rituals, emphasizing that He values heartfelt devotion and knowledge of Him over superficial worship.
